Wiener Schnitzel

Go back

Description

The famous Wiener Schnitzel is by far the most well known dish of the austrian cuisine. When well prepared, it is very tasty and of high nutritional value. Even though it is typically prepared with veal, other types of meat like pork or chicken are also a great fit for the dish. Best served with slices of lemon and a potato salad as a side dish, the Wiener Schnitzel will never fail to meet the standards of even your most critical guests.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds veal
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 4 eggs
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 cups bread crumbs

Preparation

  1. Cut the veal into steaks, about as thick as your finger.
  2. Dredge in flour.
  3. In a shallow dish, beat the eggs with 1 tablespoon oil, salt and pepper.
  4. Coat the veal with egg mixture, then with bread crumbs.
  5. Heat 1/4 cup oil in a heavy skillet over medium heat.
  6. Fry veal until golden brown, about 5 minutes on each side.
